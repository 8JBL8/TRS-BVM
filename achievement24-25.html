ROBOCON 2022

ROBOCON 2022

Team Panchjanya from BVM Engineering College recently participated in the prestigious Doordarshan Robocon India 2022 (DD Robocon) competition hosted by the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi (IIT Delhi). DD Robocon is a national competition that challenges teams of engineering students to design and build robots to complete specific tasks within a set amount of time. The competition is a part of the larger Asia-Pacific Broadcasting Union Robocon, which is an annual international competition. DD Robocon 2022 was focused on the theme "Lagori," a traditional Indian game. The participating teams were required to design and build robots that could complete the Lagori game tasks.

Team Panchjanya designed and built a robot with a simple yet effective ball-pickup mechanism that successfully picked up all six balls within the set time limit. Their optimized design and driving skills helped them perform well in all three rounds, leading them to win the Abdul Kalam Award for being the Best New Team. The award was presented by Addverb Technologies, with a prize of INR 50,000.
GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0

OxidaneX21

OxidaneX21 is a team of young engineering students from TRS BVM Student Chapter who have worked on an underwater robot, which won them the 'Underwater Robot' category in the GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0 state level robot making competition in 2021-22. The team has created a robot with wireless control over long distances, a rapidly swappable battery, a hydrodynamic design, and an analog and digital HD camera. It's a commendable achievement for these students to have developed such a sophisticated robot and win in a state-level competition.

The OxidaneX21 Underwater Robot is designed to operate underwater and is equipped with a variety of features that enable it to perform various tasks such as exploration, inspection, and search and rescue missions. The robot is wirelessly controlled from a long distance, which makes it ideal for use in remote locations or situations where direct human intervention is not possible. Its hydrodynamic design allows it to move efficiently through the water and navigate through tight spaces.

One of the most significant aspects of the OxidaneX21 robot is its ability to capture high-quality analog and digital HD camera footage. This allows it to be used for underwater research, exploration, and monitoring of marine life and habitats. The footage captured by the robot can also be used in environmental studies, oceanography, and marine biology research.

Moreover, the ability of OxidaneX21 to carry out inspections and search and rescue missions is also noteworthy. For example, the robot can be used to inspect underwater pipelines, shipwrecks, and other underwater structures. It can also be used to search for lost objects or even to locate victims in emergency situations.

The OxidaneX21 team was composed of five students from the Electronics Engineering Department and one student from the Mechanical Engineering Department of TRS BVM Student Chapter. The team members were Darshit Darji, Rutvik Valand, Vrushal Vara, Vivek Solanki, and Naitik Dalwadi. The team was mentored by Prof. (Dr.) Vinay J. Patel and Prof. (Dr.) Dipak M Patel.
GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0

Saksham

The Saksham robot is a prosthetic limb designed by a team from TRS BVM Student Chapter as part of the GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0 competition. The team members are Jay Goswami, Keivalya Pandya, Raunak Abhani and Vaidehi Shah, under the mentorship provided by Prof. (Dr.) Vinay J. Patel and Prof. Ashish Thakkar.

The Saksham robot is equipped with IoT technology, enabling it to connect to a dedicated web app for maintenance. It has an elbow socket that provides a comfortable fit, and the independent thumb motion allows for better grip and ease of use. The robot is also lightweight, making it easy to wear and move around.

Prosthetic limbs are essential for people with disabilities to regain their independence and mobility. With the Saksham robot's innovative features, it can significantly improve the quality of life for people who have lost limbs. The IoT technology can help doctors monitor the usage and performance of the prosthetic limb, enabling them to make necessary adjustments to improve its functionality further.

The team's efforts to file for design and utility patents also signify their intention to make the Saksham robot available to a wider audience. This shows their commitment to improving the lives of people with disabilities and making their innovative solution accessible to as many people as possible.

The Saksham robot is a testament to the power of technology and innovation to make a positive impact on people's lives. It is heartening to see young minds coming together to create something that can make a difference in the world.
GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0

MICROAR 1.0

MICROAR 1.0 is a robot developed by the Microbots team from TRS BVM Student Chapter, which won the Microbots category in GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0, a state-level robot making competition. The robot is designed to support object detection, IR-based control intelligence, color detection, and is lightweight and compact.

The MICROAR 1.0 robot has potential applications in various fields, including healthcare, agriculture, and disaster management. In healthcare, it can be used to navigate and perform tasks in narrow and hard-to-reach areas within the human body during medical procedures. In agriculture, it can assist farmers in monitoring crops, identifying weeds, and detecting plant diseases. In disaster management, the robot can be used to explore dangerous areas, search for victims, and gather information in hazardous environments.

The development of MICROAR 1.0 by the Microbots team showcases the potential of robotics to address real-world problems and enhance human capabilities. By developing robots like MICROAR 1.0, we can create more efficient and effective systems for various industries, leading to improved outcomes and quality of life.

The team members of MICROAR 1.0 were Heramb Dahivalkar and Pratham Barve from the Electronics and Communication Engineering Department, Indrajit Patel and Vishvas Suthar from the Mechanical Engineering Department, and Ved Bhosale, Dipesh Vadhwani, and Ishwariya Joshi from the Electronics Engineering Department. The faculty mentors were Prof. (Dr.) Vinay J. Patel and Prof. (Dr.) Mehfuza Hola.
GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0

BayMax–P5

Mohammed Amaan Shaikh, Zuber Makrani, Parth Makwana, Ayush Patel, and Hrushikesh Vegad have developed a Pipe Climbing Robot with the guidance of Prof. (Dr.) Vinay J. Patel and Prof. (Dr.) Rajiv Gandhi. The robot has some impressive features such as the ability to take turns at different angles, a live feed for pipe detection, automatic crack detection, and the capability to adapt to variable pipe diameters within the same pipe as well as different pipes.

The team has worked hard and cleared all three stages of the competition, ultimately winning the 'Pipe Climbing Robot' category and receiving a total prize money of INR 6,50,000. Their achievement doesn't stop there as they have also filed a patent for their innovative work.

The robot will be displayed at Gujarat Science City and Regional Science Centre, showcasing the team's hard work and dedication. Overall, their work and success in the competition are significant as it highlights the potential of robotics in various fields, particularly in the engineering and construction industries.
GUJCOST ROBOFEST 2.0

Mechno–B

Mechano-B is a quadruped robot designed and developed by Team Quadruped from Birla Vishvakarma Mahavidyalaya (BVM) in India. The team consisted of Jay Prajapati, Manav Thakkar, Pawan Patel, Ishan Mohite, and Parth Panchal from the Mechanical Engineering Department, under the guidance of Prof. (Dr.) Vinay J. Patel and Prof. (Dr.) Haresh Patolia.

The robot has several features that make it stand out, such as its strong, stable, and lightweight construction, with 12 degrees of freedom for flexible movement. It also has wireless control for convenient operation and an ergonomic design that is symmetric about its axis.

Team Quadruped cleared all three stages of the competition and was declared the winner of the 'Quadruped Robot' category, earning a total prize money of INR 7, 50,000. The robot will be displayed in Gujarat Science City/Regional Science Centre, and the team has also filed both design and utility patents for their innovative work

The Mechno-B quadruped robot showcases the team's creativity, technical skills, and dedication to innovation in the field of robotics. It has the potential to be used in various applications, such as exploration, surveillance, and search and rescue missions, demonstrating the significant impact of robotics on various industries and fields.

Robofest 3.0 Level 1

In October 2022, the TRS BVM Student Chapter embarked on a remarkable journey in the field of robotics by participating in GUJCOST ROBOFEST 3.0. The results, announced in April 2023, illuminated their extraordinary accomplishments during the Ideation stage. Comprising 14 dedicated student teams, each demonstrating unwavering commitment and innovative prowess, they collectively secured a substantial prize pool of ₹7,00,000 (₹50,000 per team).

These enterprising teams ventured into diverse Robot Making Categories, including Hexapod Robot, Maze Solving Robot, Self-Balancing Robot, Underwater Robot, Rover, Swarm Robots, and Home Cleaning and Serving Robot.

It is worth noting that these same 14 teams, showcasing their impressive Proof of Concept Models in July 2023, secured top positions, with individual winnings totaling ₹1,00,000 or ₹2,00,000 per team, resulting in a grand total of ₹22,00,000.

This exceptional achievement unequivocally underscores their unwavering dedication, innovative spirit, and commitment to excellence in the ever-evolving field of robotics, casting a well-deserved spotlight on the TRS BVM Student Chapter's remarkable accomplishments.
